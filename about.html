About

Duo Xu

I'm a product designer with an MFA in HCI from Parsons School of Design and 4+ years designing products across enterprise software, education technology, and AI platforms.

My background spans two environments that pull in opposite directions: fast-moving startups where I owned product direction from zero, and large-scale government systems where I had to design for constrained infrastructure, legacy workflows, and thousands of end users I would never personally meet. That combination pushed me to become a systems thinker first and a screen designer second.

I believe a strong design system is an argument about product direction. I believe a well-framed problem is worth more than a polished prototype. And I believe the best design work happens when designers, PMs, and engineers think together from the start, not in handoff.

I'm looking for a senior product design role where I can design at scale, contribute to product strategy, and push the craft forward.

How I Work

A few things that are true about how I approach design work, drawn from real projects rather than principles I aspire to.

Systems before screens

I start by understanding the underlying data model and user workflow before drawing anything. The component comes last, not first.

Research as a design tool

I run my own discovery when I can. Talking directly to users changes what I build in ways that secondhand synthesis cannot.

Constraints are the brief

Engineering constraints, compliance requirements, legacy systems: these are not obstacles. They are the actual problem to design within.

Cross-functional from day one

I bring PMs and engineers into the design process early. Alignment at the problem stage is cheaper than realignment at the spec stage.

Decisions over deliverables

A Figma file is not the output. A shared understanding of what to build and why is the output. Documentation follows from that.

Clarity over cleverness

If a user has to think about how to use something, the design has not done its job yet. Intuitive is a result, not a feature.
